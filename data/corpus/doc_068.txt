Basic Economy passengers (except on trans-Atlantic, trans-Pacific fights, flights to Canada, or flights to South America) are not permitted to carry on board a larger cabin bag. Basic Economy Fare passengers may only bring on board one personal item with maximum dimensions of 9 x 10 x 17 inches (22 x 25 x 43 cm).If you are a Basic Economy passenger and bring a full-sized cabin bag to the gate, you will be required to check it in and pay a $35+ handling fee.Items such as an umbrella, child restraint or nappy/diaper bag are also permitted by the United Airlines baggage allowance.On United Express flights, customers may need to check in larger cabin items at the gate, due to the smaller aircraft being used on these routes.United Airlines Carry On for InfantsAdults traveling with infants on their lap may bring a diaper bag, breast pump and child seat, in addition to their standard carry-on allowance.United Airlines Carry On and Musical InstrumentsThe United Airlines baggage allowance rules state that musical instruments may be carried on board, along with a personal item, and stowed in the overhead locker or under the seat in front.If traveling on a Basic Economy fare, however, the instrument can be carried instead of a personal item. If you bring an instrument to the gate that cannot be stored in the cabin, it must be checked and a $35+ handling fee must be paid.United Airlines Carry On and Bringing Pets on BoardAs part of United Airlines' carry on allowance, domesticated cats and are permitted to travel in the cabin on most flights within the US. A pet must be transported in a carrier and can be carried in addition to a carry-on bag. The cost is $150, one way. The maximum dimensions for hard-sided kennels are 17.5 x 12 x 9 inches (44 x 30 x 22 cm). The maximum dimensions for soft-sided kennels are 18 x 11 x 11 inches (46 x 28 x 28 cm). With the exception of birds, there may only be one pet per kennel, and the animal must be able to stand up and turn around comfortably.United Carry On and Prohibited ItemsCertain items cannot be carried on board the aircraft as they are considered dangerous. These include:Camping equipment containing fuel,Defense sprays,Fireworks,Firearms, Tasers.United Airlines Checked Baggage and Baggage FeesUnited Airlines' checked baggage allowance permits Economy and Basic Economy fare passengers to check into the hold bags that measure no more than 62 inches (158 cm) in total and that weigh no more than 50 lbs (23 kg).Higher fares, such as Business and First, plus active US military personnel, may carry bags up to 70 lbs (32 kg) in weight.US military personnel on official business are allowed to check luggage measuring up to 115 inches (292 cm) in total.The amount United charges for checked baggage varies according to route.On a US domestic flight with an economy ticket, a passenger would expect to pay$40 for the first checked bag and $50 for the second.On an economy fare to Europe, the first bag is free of charge and the second costs $100. On basic economy fares to Europe, the first bag costs $75 and the second costs $100.A number of routes, such as US - Dubai, permit 2 checked bags free of charge.There are increased United luggage allowances with higher fare types.United Airlines Checked Baggage for ChildrenChildren, including infants are granted the standard United checked baggage allowance, plus an allowance of one pushchair or car seat.United Airlines Checked Baggage and Excess Baggage FeesIf a bag exceeds the United Airlines baggage allowance, fees will be charged. These vary according to destination and the type of fare traveled on.For example, on an economy fare between Europe and the US, a third, and any further bag, costs $200.Overweight bags from 51 to 70 lbs (24 to 32 kg) cost $200 extra per bagOversized bags from 63 to 115 inches (160 to 292 cm) also incur a charge of $200 extra per bagOn an economy fare from Newark to Chicago, a third, and any additional bag, costs $150.Overweight bags from 51 to 70 lbs (24 to 32 kg) cost $100 extra per bag (or $200 between 71 and 100 lbs (33 to 46 kg)Oversized bags from 63 to 115 inches (160 to 292 cm) also incur a charge of $200 extra per bagAccording to the rules regarding United’s baggage allowances, all three penalties could potentially apply to one bag.Bags which exceed 115 inches (292 cm) in total and 100 lbs (45 kg) will not be accepted as checked baggagePlease note that there are both temporary and permanent excess baggage embargoes on certain routes, which restrict baggage allowances with United.United Airlines Checked Baggage and Exceptional ItemsItems that the United Airlines baggage allowance rules consider to be exceptional items, such as sports equipment, can be checked into the hold, often as standard checked baggage. There are some exceptions, however. Musical instruments can be checked into the hold, provided they are properly packaged.Oversize charges apply to musical instruments that measure 63 to 115 inches in total dimensions. Overweight charges apply to musical instruments that are over 50 lbs, but those weighing up to 165 lbs will be accepted.
