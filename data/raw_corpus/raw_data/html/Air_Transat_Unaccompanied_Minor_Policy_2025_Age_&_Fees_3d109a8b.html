Understanding the Air Transat Unaccompanied Minor Policy 2025Parents or guardians should read the following terms and conditions of service named unaccompanied minors Air Transat to make sure their child satisfies the standards for traveling alone and is ready for their journey if they are traveling without them (or with one but in a different compartment or cabin on board).Acceptance Criteria by AgeFrom At Least Seven Days to Seven Years, Inclusive: Kids must travel with another passenger who is at least 16 years old in the same cabin. 8 to 11 Years Old, Inclusive: In case parents select the unaccompanied minor VIP service for their children, they can fly alone or with an adult in a different cabin. In addition to paying the Air Transat unaccompanied minor service price, children under the age of 12 years who travel without a parent or legal guardian will be charged an adult rate. This service is not offered on flights to or from Colombia, connecting flights, or codeshare flights.12 to 17 Years Old, Inclusive: Children can travel in a different class with an adult or by themselves. Air Transat will be happy to help young passengers who are traveling alone, but they must be able to do it without the supervision of an Air Transat guardian. Parents can sign up for the unaccompanied minor VIP service for their young traveler’s peace of mind. In this instance, the child traveler will be required to pay both the VIP service price for unaccompanied kids and an adult rate. There will be fees for all services, documentation, identity requirements, and handling. This service named unaccompanied minor Air Transat is not accessible on flights to or from Colombia, or on connecting or codeshare flights.VIP Treatment for Air Transat Unaccompanied Minors (Ages 8 to 11 Years)From check-in until young travelers reach their destination, the unique VIP service guarantees that they receive individualized attention, which includes:An escort via airport proceduresTypical seat assignmentPriority boardingPriority luggage handlingFree appetizers from the bistro menuNote that this service is required for children. As instructed, the unaccompanied minors Air Transat fee is CA $100 per unaccompanied youngster, which must be paid in advance through our phone center.Actual amounts may differ and be paid in the currency of the departure airport at airports outside of Canada. A Guide to Air Transat Unaccompanied Minor TravelAt least three hours before the scheduled departure time of the Air Transat flight, the youngster must be checked in.The parent or guardian who is taking the Air Transat unaccompanied minors to the airport is required to give the entire contact information (name, complete address, area code, and phone number) of the person who will pick up the child at the airport.According to the Air Transat agent, the parent or guardian who is taking the child to the airport has to stay there until the plane takes off.To identify them as an unaccompanied minor, the youngster will be given a pouch to wear around their neck the entire journey. Further, a child’s meal from the Bistro menu can be pre-ordered up to 72 hours before the trip for flights to or from the South (except from Peru), the United States, and within Canada.Owing to the Air Transat unaccompanied minor policy 2025, presenting a photo ID card upon arrival will allow the person picking up the child to confirm that the contact details on the form completed upon departure match those on the card. Important Information Regarding Unaccompanied Minors on Air TransatChildren under the age of eighteen years who travel alone (or with just one parent or legal guardian) may need specific documents and/or formal permission from some nations.For the most recent criteria, a parent should check with the consulate of the nation where he/she plan to travel. Connecting or codeshare flights do not offer unaccompanied minor VIP service. Air Transat Unaccompanied Minor Assignment of a Free SeatIn compliance with the Air Transat unaccompanied minor rules, young tourists or children ages 13 years and under, along with their parents or guardians listed on the same family reservation, are entitled to standard seats at no cost. Families with reservations for five years or fewer passengers will have their seats clustered together by the system. Documents Required for Air Transat Unaccompanied MinorsA valid form of identification, such as a passport, birth certificate, citizenship card, permanent resident card, or Certificate of Indian Status, must be carried by minors when traveling on Air Transat flights.As per the unaccompanied minor policy for Air Transat, for the most recent documentation needs, parents should check with the travel agent or the consulate of the destination country. It is highly advised that children under the age of 18 years travel with a consent letter signed by both parents or legal guardians for every trip, regardless of whether they are traveling alone, with other people, or with just one parent.The parents’ or guardians’ addresses and phone numbers should be included in the letter. By following the Air Transat policy on unaccompanied minors, to make it easier for them to enter Canada, divorced or separated parents should have custody or legal separation papers and/or a letter of authorization.
