It is known as the Air France unaccompanied minor policy. This policy allows youngsters to travel after the age of four years. Further, it has all the rules, needed documentation, and fees mentioned. By abiding by these, the staff members of this airline guarantee that children who fly alone get safe, comfortable, and attentive care during their trip. Moreover, for parents and kids, Air France makes solo travel easy and worry-free by providing round-the-clock monitoring, priority handling, and extra help. Along with outlining all these guidelines that must be adhered to, it also explains how to make reservations for kids. What is the Air France Unaccompanied Minor Policy?Certain rules apply to unaccompanied minors on Air France under the age of eighteen who want to go alone:Minors can be referred to as UM by this airline.Reservations for minors must be made in advance.This functionality is only available for reservations booked via official channels.Children are permitted to take connecting flights.Kids Solo tickets can only be purchased by legal guardians of unaccompanied minors or by those authorized by legal guardians.Air France and partner lounges are closed to unaccompanied minors at the airport because of the facilities there, which make it impossible for Air France to keep an eye on the child all the time.The child will be considered an unaccompanied minor Air France if he/she is in a different cabin on the same flight.Air France will continue to keep an eye on the child during the entire trip, including any transfers.Children who are traveling alone are given priority if a flight is canceled or delayed.Even if overnight accommodations are necessary, the airline’s team will continue to keep a close eye on the child. Any particular measures taken to assist the child during the trip will be promptly communicated to parents or guardians. For Air France unaccompanied minors aged 4 to 11 years who are traveling alone on flights within Metropolitan France, French overseas departments and territories, and Papeete, the Kids Solo service is required and covered by the ticket fee. Children ages 5 to 11 years who are traveling to and from Nouméa, as well as those ages 5 to 14 years who are flying to and from Saint-Pierre-et-Miquelon and on other foreign flights, are also covered.Further, Air France unaccompanied minors who are older than the previously mentioned age groups can travel alone or choose to pay for Kids Solo:From the age of 12 years in Papeete, Nouméa, and French overseas departments and regions, as well as Metropolitan FranceFrom the age of 15 years on flights to Saint-Pierre-et-Miquelon and other countriesAs long as they are able to travel safely and are able to handle the emotional toll of losing loved ones while traveling, kids with a physical or mental disability are permitted to travel alone. Moreover, as long as the animal transportation regulations are followed, an unaccompanied minor can fly with a dog or a cat.
