What is the WestJet Unaccompanied Minor Policy 2025?For kids ages 8 to 17 years, WestJet Airlines is happy to provide an unaccompanied minor program for solo travelers. Parents might choose to use the Guardian fare program, which enables them to travel with their child to and from their destination at discounted rates, if their child does not fit the requirements listed below.When traveling, unaccompanied minors on WestJet must be at least eight years old.For children who are traveling alone and have turned eight years but not yet twelve years, this is a required service.For minors who are traveling alone and have turned 12 years but not yet turned 18 years, this is an optional service.This program is not available to children who:Are blind or deaf.Demand that a service dog be used.Possess an epinephrine auto-injector (such as an Epi-pen®) for travel due to an allergy.Need help taking the necessary medications.Have a condition that necessitates traveling with a familiar caregiver.Are unable to communicate in French or English, either orally or in writing.For an unaccompanied minor WestJet traveling as part of a WestJet Vacations package, the air carrier does not provide the unaccompanied minor program.Amusement or EntertainmentGuardians or parents might wish to bring along their child’s favorite video game, book, iPod, or toy. Further, please be aware that the flight crew can occasionally not allow the use of these products.WestJet Airlines Guardian Fare ProgramOn the majority of WestJet flights, parents or guardians can accompany children ages 2 to 11 years by purchasing the guardian fare.On all WestJet flights, a passenger who has turned two years but has not yet turned twelve years must be accompanied by an adult.However, a passenger who has turned twelve years old can travel alone.The guardian who purchases a guardian fare is entitled to a discounted rate.However, they must return to their departure city within 24 hours.When traveling with WestJet unaccompanied minors, guardians will sit next to or close to the child.The guardian’s return flights must be scheduled to depart at least two hours after the initial flight lands at its destination.Parents can find out the availability of Guardian fare flights and booking choices by calling WestJet between 6:00 A.M. and 5:30 P.M. MST.For the same, they should choose the “Speak with the Special Care Desk” option.WestJet’s 787 Dreamliner flights do not offer the guardian fare.Flight Availability and Limitations for WestJet Unaccompanied MinorFor WestJet unaccompanied minors, the flight needs to:Be for transportation between two cities in Canada. Unaccompanied minor service is not available on flights to the US or other foreign countries.For travels outside of Canada, parents might want to think about using the guardian fare program.Be a WestJet flight, meaning it is run by the airline.The airline partners’ flights are not eligible for unaccompanied minor programs.Not necessarily connecting to another flight (as several flight numbers suggest).Not reach the destination before 10:00 P.m. or depart before 05:30 A.M.Based on the WestJet unaccompanied minor policy 2025, the term “red-eye” refers to one that does not occur overnight.Please be aware that reservations using Air Miles or RBC Points cannot be made for unaccompanied minors.What is the WestJet Unaccompanied Minor Fee?On WestJet Airlines flights, at the time of booking, a USD 100 charge will be collected for each child. Assistance with check-in, flight monitoring, and transfer to the chosen guardian are all included in this unaccompanied minors WestJet fee. Checking the most recent rates is advised because the amount may vary based on the kind of flight.What is the Necessary Identification for WestJet Unaccompanied Minor?WestJet Airlines needs the child’s entire name as it appears on their identification, date of birth, and gender in order to book travel for an unaccompanied minor.Additionally, it needs the guardians driving their child to and from the airport to provide their full names (as on official identification), residences, and mobile phone numbers (or other contact information).Owing to the WestJet unaccompanied minor rules, the air operator needs a photocopy (not the original) of the child’s birth certificate or another official document proving the child’s gender and date of birth during check-in.In addition to being needed for the return trip, the photocopy will be sent with the child.When the child arrives, the person picking them up must be at least eighteen years old and must show one form of official identification with a photo.A WestJet unaccompanied minor form will be emailed to the person who booked the flight or flights after the unaccompanied minor booking is complete.As per the unaccompanied minor policy for WestJet, this paperwork is necessary for the child’s journey in both directions.Kindly fill out the form before one arrives at the airport.
