In addition to this, children aged more than 5 years can travel with this air carrier. For the same, the airline has an unaccompanied minor service. For kids traveling alone, Air Canada unaccompanied minor policy provides a safe and nurturing travel environment. From airport check-in until destination arrival, this service offers round-the-clock supervision. Further, young passengers are assisted by committed employees during boarding, in-flight monitoring, and disembarkation. Hence, this service demonstrates dedication to trustworthy, kid-friendly travel and is required for children ages 8 to 11 years and optional for those ages 12 to 17 years. Rules of the Air Canada Unaccompanied Minor PolicyThese are the rules that travelers must adhere to while making reservations with Air Canada for their child as an unaccompanied minor.If the child is traveling on one of the following, he or she can use the unaccompanied minors on Air Canada:A flight that Air Canada or Air Canada Rouge operates nonstop.Jazz’s nonstop flight on Air Canada Express. Air Canada unaccompanied minors must purchase an adult ticket.Passengers under the age of twelve years can only be accompanied by adults who are at least 16 years old. Bookings for the service can be made through the travel agent or Air Canada reservation staff.Minors traveling alone are not allowed to change their flights on Air Canada.It is recommended that children sit in the final three rows of the airplane.The crew members can then take care of the needs of unaccompanied minor on Air Canada.It is not possible to use the unaccompanied minor service on the day of departure.In the event of a flight disruption, the parent or responsible adult must stay at the airport until the flight has taken off. Since Air Canada is unable to handle all of the duties required to move a kid through a connecting airport, the unaccompanied kid service cannot be bought for codeshare or connecting flights. Additionally, unaccompanied kids can find it challenging to make their way through connections; this difficulty may increase if there is a flight disruption at the connection point.Restrictions on the Air Canada Unaccompanied Minor PolicyBefore scheduling the Air Canada unaccompanied minor service, parents should be aware of the following limitations:Without prior physician approval, a child with any other medical condition cannot travel alone.The child is not permitted to travel alone if they are:Deaf or blindHas additional severe allergies or is extremely sensitive to peanuts or peanut-derived items.Extremely sensitive to dogs and catsNeeds extra care or medication during the flight because of a medical condition, such as allergies, but not exclusively.Likely to have a negative reaction to flying without a responsible adult in their lifeAir Canada prohibits unaccompanied minors from traveling in the cabin with pets.Codeshare and connected flights do not offer the Air Canada unaccompanied minors service.Checking in online is not allowed. Children must go to the airport and approach the Air Canada check-in desk.What are the Air Canada Age Requirements for Unaccompanied Minors?Air Canada makes certain that its unaccompanied minor age limitations are fully understood by its passengers.
