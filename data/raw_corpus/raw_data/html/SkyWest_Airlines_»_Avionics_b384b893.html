Support Invest: SKYW Avionics Career Guide apply now Become a SkyWest Avionics TechnicianSkyWest is currently hiring qualified individuals to work as Avionics Technicians. As an Avionics Technician, you will be responsible to perform all maintenance functions and effectively troubleshoot the SkyWest fleet of CRJ200, CRJ550, CRJ700, CRJ900 and E175 aircraft. Requirements Graduated from an accredited avionics school Military training in avionics or electronics Eighteen months (or more) experience in the avionics industry Associate degree or higher in electronics A&P certificate with previous avionics experience Be able to climb, bend, kneel, crawl and stoop frequently in confined spaces Possess basic Avionics skills Own the minimum set of tools Have acceptable skills to read and understand blueprints and schematics Be able to routinely lift 75 lbs. with or without accommodation Have completed Avionics line OJT training before working an independent shift Have a stable employment history; SkyWest Airlines requires a background check on all employees Possess excellent decision-making and communication skills Have a positive attitude and work well with other employees Hold an FCC GROL license or be able to obtain one within six months from hire date Maintenance Bases and Line Stations Minimum Tool Set 1/2” Breaker Bar 1 1/8” Deep Socket, 1/2” Drive Flashlight Small Set of Punches 10" or 12" Adjustable Crescent Type Wrench (Recommend Knipex Plier Wrench) Set Ignition Wrenches Set of ALLEN WRENCHES Center Punch VISE GRIPS PHILLIPS Offset Screwdriver (or Reversible Ratcheting Bit Wrench) #2 PHILLIPS Stubbie Screwdriver Safety Wire Pliers (Recommended 6”) Set of Picks (For O-Rings) 6" Needle Nose Vise Grips Screw Extractor Set (Easy Out) 1 1/4" Wrench, Combination Box -Open End 12 Point 1/4" Drive Ratchet 12 Point Shallow and Deep Sockets 3/16” Through 9/16” With Extensions 3/8' Through 1" Wrenches, Combination Box -Open End 12 Point Magnet Ratchet Adapters: 1/4”-3/8”, 3/8”-1/4”, 3/8”-1/2”, 1/2”-3/8”, 1/2”Female-3/4”Male Duckbill Pliers Ball Peen Hammer (8-16 oz.) 3/8" Drive Ratchet 12 Point Sockets 3/8" Through 7/8" and Extensions Plastic or Soft Mallet Needle Nose Pliers Standard Feeler Gauge Stake On or Terminal Crimping Pliers 7/8"x1/2" Drive Socket DEEP Inspection Mirror (DENTAL) & 2" Round Common Offset Screwdriver (or Reversible Ratcheting Bit Wrench) Tool Box - Low Profile 1 1/8" Wrench, Combination Box -Open End 12 Point Taps: 6-32, 8-32, 10-32 Cotter Pin Puller Rechargeable Battery Powered Screw Gun with Adjustable Clutch. Diagonal Cutters Multi-Tool (Leatherman) Slip Joint or Water Pump Pliers Print Tool List Bench Technicians To qualify for the bench override in base locations and work as an avionics bench technician, one of the following prerequisites must be met: Graduated from an accredited avionics school Military training in avionics/electronics Associate degree or higher in electronics and Hold Qty 2 vendor level 3 equipment repair certificates in addition to 12 months avionics bench repair experience and have an avionics bench position assigned A&P and FCC GROL certificates with Qty 2 vendor level 3 equipment repair certificates in addition to 12 months avionics bench repair experience, as well as provide evidence of knowledge in electronics fundamentals and have an avionics bench position assigned Training, Uniforms and Pay SkyWest Airlines provides in-house training on the equipment we operate. During training, avionics technicians are paid their hourly rate based on a 40-hour workweek. If the employee has to attend training in a location away from their home base, SkyWest Airlines will provide hotel accommodations and a per diem for meal expenses. SkyWest Airlines supplies and cleans avionics technician uniforms at no cost to the employee. Pay is based on previous experience and will be discussed during the interview. Essential Duties & Responsibilities SkyWest Avionic Techs comply with FAA regulations as they work to solve routine and non-routine technical issues.
