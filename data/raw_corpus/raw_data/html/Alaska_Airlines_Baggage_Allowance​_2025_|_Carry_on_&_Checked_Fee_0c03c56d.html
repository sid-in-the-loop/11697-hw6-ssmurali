Does Alaska Airlines Allow Carry on Baggage on Flights? 1 personal item, such as a laptop computer, briefcase, or pocketbook, is permitted on Alaska Airlines flights. The sum of these three measurements (length, height, and width) cannot exceed 45 inches. This air operator does not take glass kayaks. Are Perishable Items Allowed on Alaska Airlines Flights?All perishable goods must be packaged correctly. Raw game meat and fish can only be brought as luggage when they are wrapped in a leak-proof container. It should be packaged to last at least 48 hours in transit without refrigeration. Further, each customer can bring gel ice up to 5.5 pounds of dry ice. Moreover, wet ice is provided. Note: On planes operated by Alaska Airlines, Styrofoam is prohibited. Big Game Trophies Alaska Airlines only allows the checking of horns and antlers. Provided that, it should be packaged appropriately. The item needs to be wrapped in a sturdy plastic bag or shrink wrap. To avoid punctures, antler tips need to be cushioned and covered. Raw meat, blood, other residue, and scents must be absent from antlers. A single permitted dimension cannot be more than 70 inches long or tall, 24 inches broad, or 38 inches deep. Oversized/overweight items weighing more than 50 pounds are subject to the excess charges. A total dimension of no more than 115 linear inches is permitted. Animal racks and antlers that weigh more than 100 pounds or 115 inches are not permitted as checked baggage. Each flight is limited to 1 animal or rack as part of Alaska Airlines baggage allowance 2025​. Based on available space, extra animals can be permitted. Does Alaska Airlines Allow Firearms on Board?Currently, firearms can only be transported as checked luggage. To travel with a firearm, a passenger must be at least eighteen years old. It is required that firearms be unloaded and transported in a hard-sided and secured container. To prevent unintentional or unauthorized access, the container must be totally secure as per the regulations of Alaska Airlines international baggage allowance​. In certain instances, several locks can be necessary to guarantee that the handgun and its contents are totally unreachable and unopenable. Note: The airline will not accept locks that do not totally prohibit unwanted entry. Alaska Airlines advises packing tiny comfort items in carry-on suitcases. These include tiny nibbles, a beloved pillow, a security blanket, tiny toys, crayons, tiny electronics, and coloring books. A child’s stroller and car seat are allowed as Alaska Airlines baggage allowance​ 2025 at no additional cost. Travelers can either wait until they get to the gate area or check these goods with their other luggage. Is Wheelchair Allowed on Flights of Alaska Airlines?All kinds of personal wheelchairs are transported by Alaska Airlines on its flights. These consist of electric or battery-powered, electric carts or scooters, folding, collapsible, and more. These need to be within the Alaska Airlines baggage allowance 2025​ limits of the aircraft’s authorized storage area. If the wheelchair cannot be safely stowed in the approved storage closet, then the airline will transport it to the cargo compartment at no extra charge. Spillable Batteries: According to federal HAZMAT safety requirements, the battery must be taken out when a mobility assistance is powered by a spillable battery. It must be out in a protected battery box. Non-Spillable Batteries: According to the Alaska Airline carry on baggage allowance​​ terms, one “spare” non-spillable battery is allowed per guest. The words “NON-SPILLABLE” OR “NON-SPILLABLE BATTERY” must be permanently marked on the exterior of a non-spillable battery by the manufacturer. These batteries are also shipped as checked luggage. Lithium-Ion (Li-ion) Batteries: Mobility aids with batteries that are more than 300 watt-hours or that are completely encased and difficult to remove will not be permitted for transportation. It needs to be simple to detach and carry in the passenger cabin as separate carry-on luggage. This is because it is necessary to prevent short circuits at the battery terminals.
